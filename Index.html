How to Pray Namaz

🕌 How to Pray Namaz (Salah)

1. Niyyah (Intention)

Make intention in your heart for which Salah you are praying (e.g., Fajr, Zuhr).

2. Takbir (Start the Prayer)

اللهُ أَكْبَر

Allahu Akbar

Allah is the Greatest

3. Dua al-Istiftah (Opening Supplication)

سُبْحَانَكَ اللّهُمَّ وَبِحَمْدِكَ، وَتَبَارَكَ اسْمُكَ، وَتَعَالَى جَدُّكَ، وَلَا إِلٰهَ غَيْرُكَ

Subhanaka Allahumma wa bihamdika wa tabarakasmuka wa ta’ala jadduka wa la ilaha ghayruka

Glory is to You, O Allah, and praise is to You; blessed is Your Name, and exalted is Your Majesty; there is no god but You.

4. Surah Al-Fatiha

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ...

Alhamdu lillahi rabbil 'alamin...

All praise is for Allah—Lord of all worlds...

5. A Short Surah (e.g., Surah Ikhlas)

قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ...

Qul huwa Allahu ahad...

Say, He is Allah, the One...

6. Ruku (Bowing)

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيم

Subhana Rabbiyal Azeem

Glory is to my Lord, the Most Great

7. Stand (Qawmah)

سَمِعَ اللَّهُ لِمَنْ حَمِدَه

Sami' Allahu liman hamidah

Allah hears those who praise Him

8. Sujood (Prostration)

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الأَعْلَى

Subhana Rabbiyal A'la

Glory is to my Lord, the Most High

9. Sitting Between Two Sujood

رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي

Rabbighfir li

O my Lord, forgive me

10. Tashahhud (After 2 Rakats or in Last Sitting)

التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلَّهِ وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ...

At-tahiyyatu lillahi was-salawatu wat-tayyibat...

All greetings, prayers, and goodness are for Allah...

11. Salawat (Durood)

اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ...

Allahumma salli 'ala Muhammadin wa 'ala ali Muhammad...

O Allah, send peace upon Muhammad and the family of Muhammad...

12. Dua (Optional)

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً...

Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanah...

Our Lord, give us in this world that which is good...

13. Tasleem (Ending the Prayer)

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّهِ

As-salamu Alaikum wa Rahmatullah

Peace and mercy of Allah be upon you

About Ramzan Month

10 Lines About Ramzan Month 🕌

  • Ramzan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar.
  • It is the holiest month for Muslims around the world.
  • During Ramzan, Muslims fast from dawn (Fajr) to sunset (Maghrib).
  • Fasting means no eating, drinking, or bad behavior during daylight hours.
  • It is a time of worship, prayer, and reading the Quran.
  • Muslims believe that the Quran was first revealed during Ramzan.
  • The fast teaches self-control, patience, and empathy for the poor.
  • Special nightly prayers called Taraweeh are performed in this month.
  • The last ten days of Ramzan are considered especially holy.
  • Ramzan ends with a festival called Eid-ul-Fitr, a day of joy and gratitude.
Life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ
Life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ 🌙🕌

🌟 Birth and Early Life

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was born in Mecca in the year 570 CE, in the tribe of Quraysh. His father Abdullah died before his birth, and his mother Amina passed away when he was only 6 years old. He was raised by his grandfather Abdul Muttalib and later by his uncle Abu Talib.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Youth and Honesty

As a young man, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was known as Al-Ameen (the trustworthy) and As-Sadiq (the truthful). He was known for his honesty, good character, and kindness to the poor and weak. He worked as a shepherd and later became a successful merchant.

💍 Marriage to Khadijah (RA)

At the age of 25, he married Khadijah (RA), a noble and wealthy businesswoman. She was 15 years older than him, and their marriage was based on love, respect, and trust. Khadijah (RA) was the first person to accept Islam.

📖 First Revelation

At the age of 40, while meditating in the Cave of Hira, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ received the first revelation from Allah ﷻ through Angel Jibreel (Gabriel). The first verse revealed was "Read in the name of your Lord who created."

📢 Spreading the Message of Islam

He began preaching the message of one God (Allah), kindness, justice, and good character. Many people rejected and persecuted him, especially the leaders of Quraysh, but he continued with patience and prayer.

🏃‍♂️ Migration to Madinah (Hijrah)

After years of persecution, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and his followers migrated to Madinah. This event is known as Hijrah and marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar. In Madinah, he established the first Islamic state based on peace and justice.

⚔️ Battles and Struggles

The Muslims faced several battles like Badr, Uhud, and Khandaq. Despite being outnumbered, they remained strong through faith in Allah. The Prophet ﷺ always emphasized peace and avoided conflict unless necessary.

🤝 Treaty of Hudaybiyyah and Conquest of Makkah

The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah was a turning point. Later, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ returned to Makkah with a large group of Muslims and conquered it peacefully. He forgave his enemies and cleaned the Kaaba of idols.

🕋 Farewell Pilgrimage and Last Sermon

In his final years, the Prophet ﷺ performed his only Hajj (pilgrimage). During it, he delivered his famous last sermon, teaching equality, brotherhood, women's rights, and following the Quran and Sunnah.

🕊️ Passing of the Prophet ﷺ

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ passed away at the age of 63 in Madinah. His final words were, "O Allah, (with) the highest companionship." His teachings continue to guide over a billion Muslims around the world today.

Makkah & Madinah Sharif
🌙 Makkah & Madinah Sharif 🕋🕌

🕋 About Makkah Sharif

Makkah is the holiest city in Islam and the birthplace of our beloved Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. It is located in modern-day Saudi Arabia. Every year, millions of Muslims travel to Makkah to perform Hajj and Umrah.

The most sacred site in Makkah is the **Kaaba**, located inside **Masjid al-Haram**. It is the Qibla (direction) that Muslims face during prayer.

📍 Important Places in Makkah:

  • Kaaba – The house of Allah
  • Masjid al-Haram – Largest mosque in the world
  • Hajr-e-Aswad (Black Stone)
  • Well of Zamzam – Holy water gifted by Allah
  • Mount Safa and Marwah – Where Hajar (RA) ran in search of water
  • Cave of Hira – Where first revelation was received

🕌 About Madinah Sharif

Madinah (also called Al-Madinah Al-Munawwarah) is the second holiest city in Islam. It is where the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ migrated during Hijrah and established the first Islamic society.

The most important place in Madinah is **Masjid an-Nabawi**, the Prophet's mosque. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is buried there, and millions of Muslims visit his grave with love and respect.

📍 Important Places in Madinah:

  • Masjid an-Nabawi – Second holiest mosque in Islam
  • Rawdah – Garden of Paradise inside Masjid an-Nabawi
  • Jannatul Baqi – Burial place of many companions and family members
  • Quba Mosque – The first mosque built in Islam
  • Mount Uhud – Site of the Battle of Uhud
  • Masjid Qiblatain – Mosque where Qibla changed toward Kaaba

🕊️ Importance in Islam

  • Makkah is mentioned in the Qur’an many times as a sacred place.
  • Madinah was chosen by Allah as the city of peace and guidance.
  • Visiting both cities (Makkah for Hajj/Umrah and Madinah to visit the Prophet ﷺ) is highly recommended for every Muslim.
  • Both cities are free from war and protected by Allah.

About Jamaat (Congregational Prayer)

🕌 Jamaat (Congregational Prayer)

Written by: Md Mahfuz from Supaul, Bihar

📘 What is Jamaat?

Jamaat refers to Muslims gathering together to offer Salah (Namaz) in congregation. It is a great Sunnah and highly recommended in Islam. Performing Salah in Jamaat brings more reward and unity among Muslims.

🌟 Importance of Jamaat

  • Brings unity and brotherhood
  • Promotes discipline and punctuality
  • Increases rewards (27 times more than praying alone)
  • Helps know the condition of the community
  • Shows the strength of the Muslim Ummah

📜 Hadiths about Jamaat

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said:
“Prayer in congregation is twenty-seven times more rewarding than a prayer offered by oneself.”
— (Bukhari & Muslim)
The Prophet ﷺ also said:
“If people knew the reward for performing prayer in Jamaat, they would go to it even if they had to crawl.”
— (Bukhari)

🏠 Importance of the Masjid

The mosque is the center of the Muslim community. Performing Jamaat prayer in the mosque is a sign of a strong believer. The mosque is not just a place for prayer but also for knowledge, peace, and unity.

✅ Benefits of Jamaat

  • Spiritual connection with Allah
  • Forgiveness of sins
  • Unity with fellow Muslims
  • Peace of mind and heart

📅 Daily Jamaat Times

Jamaat is performed five times a day:

  • Fajr – Early morning
  • Dhuhr – Noon
  • Asr – Afternoon
  • Maghrib – Just after sunset
  • Isha – Night
🤲 Let us always try to attend Jamaat and encourage others to do the same.

🌙 Islamic Festivals

🌙 Islamic Festivals 🕌

Islam has many beautiful festivals and occasions that bring joy, unity, and a chance to increase in worship. These days are spiritually significant and celebrated by Muslims around the world. Let's explore the main Islamic festivals.

🕌 Eid-ul-Fitr 🎉

Eid-ul-Fitr is celebrated after the holy month of Ramadan. It marks the end of fasting. Muslims wear new clothes 👕, offer special prayer 🕌, give charity called Fitrana, and meet friends and family. It is a day of joy, food 🍽️, and blessings.

🕋 Eid-ul-Adha 🐑

Eid-ul-Adha, also known as the Festival of Sacrifice, is celebrated in the month of Dhul-Hijjah. It reminds us of the obedience of Prophet Ibrahim (A.S) who was ready to sacrifice his son. Muslims around the world sacrifice animals 🐄🐐 and distribute meat to the poor. Special prayers are offered in the morning.

🌙 Ramadan (Ramzan) 🕯️

Ramadan is the most sacred month in Islam. Muslims fast from sunrise to sunset 🍽️, offer more prayers, read the Quran 📖, and do charity. It teaches self-control, patience, and closeness to Allah. It ends with the celebration of Eid-ul-Fitr.

🕊️ Milad-un-Nabi ﷺ

This day celebrates the birth of our beloved Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. Muslims hold special gatherings, recite naat and seerat, and learn more about his life and teachings. It is a day of sending peace and blessings upon the Prophet. 📿

☪️ Muharram & Ashura

Muharram is the first month of the Islamic calendar. The 10th day is called Ashura. It is a day of fasting and remembrance of the martyrdom of Imam Hussain (R.A) at Karbala. Muslims reflect on sacrifice, truth, and standing against injustice. ⚔️

🧕 Islamic Unity & Brotherhood 🤝

All Islamic festivals teach us love, care, kindness, and brotherhood. They bring communities together and remind us to thank Allah for His blessings. 🤲

🩺 Khatna in Islam (Circumcision)

🩺 Khatna (Circumcision) in Islam

Khatna (خَتْن) means **circumcision**, the removal of the foreskin of the male organ. It is a religious practice in Islam, performed during childhood as a part of the Sunnah of Prophet Ibrahim (A.S) and practiced by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

🌟 What is Khatna?

Khatna is the removal of the skin covering the tip of the male organ. In Islam, it is an important Sunnah, and most Muslim boys undergo khatna at an early age (usually between 1 to 10 years).

🕋 Importance in Islam

Prophet Ibrahim (A.S) performed his own khatna at the age of 80 as an act of obedience to Allah. It is considered a Sunnah Muakkadah (strongly recommended Sunnah) and part of **fitrah** (natural practices).

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said:

"Five things are part of fitrah: circumcision, shaving the pubic hair, cutting the nails, plucking the underarm hair, and trimming the mustache."
— (Sahih Bukhari & Muslim)

🩺 Medical Benefits

  • Better cleanliness and hygiene 🧼
  • Reduces risk of infections and diseases 🧬
  • Helps prevent urinary tract infections in early life 💧
  • Reduces risk of some sexually transmitted diseases 🚫

🧒 At What Age is Khatna Done?

Most Muslims prefer to perform khatna in childhood, but it is valid at any age before adulthood. Some do it after 7 days of birth, while others wait until the child grows a little. It depends on the family's tradition.

💡 Spiritual Meaning

Khatna is a sign of purification. It shows that the Muslim is following the Sunnah and staying clean as required for Salah and daily life. It is an identity of Muslim boys.

👨‍⚕️ Who Performs It?

Khatna is performed by a trained doctor or a person experienced in circumcision. It is usually done with care and safety, sometimes under local anesthesia in a hospital or clinic.

✅ Summary

  • Khatna is Sunnah of Prophet Ibrahim (A.S) and practiced by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ
  • It is part of Muslim identity and hygiene
  • Medical and spiritual benefits
  • Usually done in childhood by doctors
✅ Benefits of Khatna (Circumcision)

🩺 Benefits of Khatna (Circumcision)

Khatna, or circumcision, is a deeply respected Sunnah in Islam. It holds not only spiritual value but also provides many medical, hygienic, and social benefits. It is a symbol of purity and cleanliness.

🕌 Spiritual Benefits

  • ✅ Following the Sunnah of Prophet Ibrahim (A.S)
  • ✅ Part of the five fitrah acts taught by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ
  • ✅ Increases cleanliness required for Salah (prayer) and ibadah
  • ✅ Sign of obedience to Allah’s commandments
  • ✅ Acts as identity of Muslim boys and men

🧬 Medical Benefits

  • 🧼 Improves hygiene by making it easier to clean the private area
  • 🦠 Reduces chances of urinary tract infections (UTIs)
  • 🛡️ Lowers risk of penile infections and inflammation
  • ❌ Decreases the risk of sexually transmitted diseases (STDs)
  • 🧫 Reduces chances of certain types of cancer
  • 💉 Often performed under medical safety with quick healing

👥 Social & Personal Benefits

  • 🤝 Increases acceptance in Muslim community as a complete believer
  • 🔖 Often considered part of male initiation in society
  • 🧘 Boosts self-esteem in cleanliness and manhood
  • 🌍 Common across many cultures and religions (not just Islam)

📖 Hadith Reference

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said:
“Five practices are characteristics of the fitrah: circumcision, shaving the pubic region, cutting the nails, plucking the armpit hair, and trimming the mustache.”
— (Sahih Bukhari & Muslim)

📌 Summary

  • 🕋 Khatna is a part of Islamic purity and identity
  • 🧬 Brings health benefits proven by doctors and science
  • ✅ Done with care, it protects both body and soul
